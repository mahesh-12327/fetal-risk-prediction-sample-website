Fetal heart monitoring

Cardiac Fetal control monitors the heart rhythm (fetus) of your infant. This helps the primary care professional to see how well the infant is. In the latter stages of pregnancy, the primary care provider will do a fetal heart rhythm check. The natural heart rate of fetuses varies from 110 to 155 bpm. This may be anywhere 5-24 beats per minute. The fetal heart rate varies the baby responds to uterine condition. An erratic fetal heart rate means the baby doesn't get enough oxygen or any other problem.

A.External fetal heart monitoring
We use a system to hear the baby's heartbeat via patient abdomen and monitor it. An ultrasonic Doppler-system is one type of monitor. It's also used at prenatal appointments to monitor the baby's heart rate. It is also used to monitor fetal heart rate during pregnancy. During gestation and delivery, the physician can alsomonitor the baby's heart rate daily. Your abdomen is fastened to the ultrasonic (transducer) probe to do so. It brings the sound of your baby's heart inside a computer. Your baby's heart rhythm and pattern is projected on a monitor,and written on paper.







B.Internal fetal heart monitoring
We place a wire (electrode) which is placed on the infant's scalp. From the baby the wire travels through the cervix. The computer binds. This approach provides stronger readings, as it does not influence such aspects as movement. But this can be done by removing and opening the cervix (amniotic sac) from the fluid-filled membrane that surrounds the baby during pregnancy. The provider can use internal checking if the external checking does not provide a good reading. And your doctor can use this device during pregnancy, to take a closer look at your infant.
During delivery the health care provider must monitor the baby's uterine contraction and heart rate. The doctor will note how often you feel contractions & how long one lasts. As both heart rate and contraction are concurrently recorded as fetals, these results can be viewed and compared.
During internal fetal heart monitoring, doctor can test the pressure inside the uterus. Doctor would then insert a tube across the cervix and into the uterus to do so. The catheter should forward a reading of uterine pressure to a monitor.

Need of fetal heart monitoring
Monitoring of the fetal heart rate is useful when a woman has a high-risk pregnancy. If patient has diabetes or a high bp, the kid is at high risk. It is also high risk when the baby is not growing or progressing as it should. Monitoring of fetal heart rates may be used to test how early laboratory drugs impact the infant. These are medicines which help prevent the work from beginning too early. Many studies can also take advantage of fetal heart rate control including:

A.Nonstress test : This tracks fetal heart rate as the baby moves.
B.Contraction stress test : This tracks fetal heart rhythm between uterine contractions.Contractions are initiated using drugs or other methods.
C.A biophysical profile : It is a combination of nonstress and ultrasound tests.
